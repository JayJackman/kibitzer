# SAYC — Standard American Yellow Card

## What Is SAYC?

SAYC is a set of bidding agreements created by the American Contract Bridge League (ACBL). It is the required system for Standard Yellow Card events and serves as the default for casual or online partnerships. The official reference is the ACBL SAYC System Booklet (SP-3), revised January 2006.

## Core System Features

- **Five-card majors** in all seats
- **Strong artificial 2C** opening (22+ HCP or playing equivalent)
- **Weak two-bids** in diamonds, hearts, and spades (5–11 HCP, 6-card suit)
- **15–17 HCP 1NT** opening
- **Non-forcing Stayman** and **Jacoby transfers** over 1NT
- **Standard Blackwood** (not Roman Key Card) and **Gerber** over notrump
- **Negative doubles** through 2S (per the SAYC convention card and booklet)
- **Michaels cue bids** and **Unusual 2NT**
- **Jacoby 2NT** game-forcing major raise
- **Limit raises**
- **Weak jump shifts** in competition
- **Jordan 2NT** (limit raise after takeout double)

## Hand Evaluation

### High Card Points (HCP)

| Honor | Points |
|-------|--------|
| Ace | 4 |
| King | 3 |
| Queen | 2 |
| Jack | 1 |

### Support Points (Dummy Points)

When raising partner's suit as responder, add for shortness in side suits:

| Holding | Points |
|---------|--------|
| Void | 5 |
| Singleton | 3 |
| Doubleton | 1 |

Do not count side-suit honors as both HCP and support points.

### Bergen Points (Opener Re-evaluation After a Fit)

When partner has raised your suit, re-evaluate using Bergen's method (Marty Bergen, *Points Schmoints*). Shortness only counts once a fit is confirmed:

| Factor | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Singleton (side suit) | +2 |
| Void (side suit) | +4 |
| Each trump beyond the 5th | +1 (e.g., 6-card suit = +1, 7-card = +2) |
| 4-card or 5-card side suit | +1 |

Start with HCP, then add the above. This replaces length points once a fit is established.

**Why not just use support points?** Support points (dummy points) are designed for the hand that will be dummy — the responder raising partner. Bergen points are for the hand that will be declarer — the opener whose suit was raised. The adjustments differ because declarer values long trumps and side suits for tricks, while dummy values shortness for ruffs.

### Length Points

Add 1 point for the fifth card in any suit longer than four cards, and at least 1 more for the sixth, seventh, etc.

### Opening Guidelines

- **Rule of 20** (1st/2nd seat): Open if HCP + length of two longest suits >= 20
- **Rule of 15** (4th seat): Open if HCP + number of spades >= 15
- **3rd seat**: Light openings are permitted
