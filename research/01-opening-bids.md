# Opening Bids

## One-Level Suit Openings

### General Requirements

- **HCP**: 12–21
- Open the **longest suit**
- With two suits of equal length (5-5 or 6-6): open the **higher-ranking** suit
- With two 4-card suits: open the **higher-ranking** suit (exception: 4-4 minors, open 1D)
- Minimum opening strength: typically 12+ HCP, or meeting the Rule of 20

### Major Suit Openings (1H, 1S)

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| HCP | 12–21 |
| Suit length | 5+ cards |
| Priority | Open the longer major; with 5-5 in majors, open 1S |

Five-card majors are required in all seats.

### Minor Suit Openings (1C, 1D)

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| HCP | 12–21 |
| 1D suit length | 4+ cards (3 only with 4-4 in the majors and 3-2 in the minors) |
| 1C suit length | 3+ cards (catch-all when no other opening applies) |

#### Minor Suit Selection Rules

| Hand Shape | Open |
|------------|------|
| Longer minor | The longer minor |
| 4-4 in minors | 1D |
| 3-3 in minors | 1C |
| 4-4 in majors, 3-2 minors | 1D (even with only 3 diamonds) |

### Choosing Between Suits — Priority Summary

1. **One 5+ card suit**: Open that suit
2. **Two 5-card suits**: Open the higher-ranking suit
3. **Two 4-card suits**: Open the higher-ranking suit
4. **4-4 in minors**: Open 1D
5. **3-3 in minors**: Open 1C
6. **4-4-4-1 shape**: Open the middle of the three 4-card suits (equivalently, the suit ranking below the singleton)

#### 4-4-4-1 Shape

| Singleton | Open |
|-----------|------|
| Spade | 1H |
| Heart | 1D |
| Diamond | 1C |
| Club | Open the middle suit (1H) |

**Ambiguity note**: With a singleton club, the "suit below the singleton" logic wraps to 1S, but the more common SAYC guidance is to open the middle of the three 4-card suits, which is 1H. This is one area where SAYC sources vary slightly.

## 1NT Opening

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| HCP | 15–17 |
| Shape | Balanced: 4-3-3-3, 4-4-3-2, or 5-3-3-2 |
| Restrictions | No singleton, no void |
| 5-card suit | Permitted (including a 5-card major) |

---

## 2C Opening (Strong, Artificial, Forcing)

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| HCP | 22+ HCP, or the playing equivalent with a very strong unbalanced hand |
| Meaning | Artificial — says nothing about clubs |
| Forcing | Partner may not pass (unless there is an intervening bid) |

### Opener's Rebid After 2C

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 2NT | 22–24 HCP, balanced (Stayman and transfers apply) |
| 3NT | 25–27 HCP, balanced |
| Suit at cheapest level | Natural, 5+ cards, forcing (to 3 of a major or 4 of a minor) |
| Jump in suit | Self-supporting suit, game forcing |

## Weak Two Openings (2D, 2H, 2S)

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| HCP | 5–11 |
| Suit length | 6-card suit |
| Suit quality | Reasonable quality (typically 2 of top 3 honors or 3 of top 5) |
| Restrictions | No void, no outside 4-card major |

### Notes

- 2C is reserved for the strong artificial opening — there is no weak 2C
- In 3rd seat, a 5-card suit or a poor 7-card suit may occasionally be opened as a weak two
- Vulnerability should influence aggressiveness

## Three-Level Preemptive Openings (3C, 3D, 3H, 3S)

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| HCP | Less than opening strength (typically ~5–10) |
| Suit length | 7-card suit |
| Suit quality | Reasonable — follows the Rule of 2 and 3 |

### Rule of 2 and 3

- **Vulnerable**: Expect to go down no more than 2 tricks (assuming partner has no useful cards)
- **Not vulnerable**: Expect to go down no more than 3 tricks

Little or no outside strength — defensive values are concentrated in the long suit. If the hand is strong enough to open at the 1-level, open at the 1-level instead.

---

## 3NT Opening

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| HCP | 25–27 |
| Shape | Balanced: 4-3-3-3, 4-4-3-2, or 5-3-3-2 |
| Opening | Direct — bid 3NT as the opening bid |

The SAYC convention card and booklet list 3NT as a direct opening showing 25–27 HCP balanced. It may also be reached via 2C—2D—3NT (showing the same range). Both paths are valid.

**Note**: The gambling 3NT (solid 7-card minor with little outside) is **not** part of standard SAYC.

---

## Four-Level Preemptive Openings (4C, 4D, 4H, 4S)

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| HCP | Less than opening strength |
| Suit length | Typically 8+ cards (7 with very good shape) |
| 4H/4S | Preemptive with a long major, approximately 8 playing tricks |
| 4C/4D | Preemptive with an 8+ card minor (rare) |

Follows the same preemptive philosophy as 3-level openings, one level higher.
