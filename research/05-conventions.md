# Conventions

## Stayman

### When It Applies

- Over 1NT opening (15–17)
- Over 2NT opening (20–21)
- Over 2C—2D—2NT (22–24)
- Over 2C—2D—3NT (25–27)

### Bid

2C over 1NT (or 3C over 2NT) is **non-forcing Stayman** — the bidding may stop in two of a suit.

### Requirements

- **8+ HCP** to use Stayman (invitational or game-forcing values)
- May be used with fewer HCP when holding 4-4-4-1 shape (planning to pass any response)
- Should **not** be used with 4-3-3-3 or 3-4-3-3 flat hands — prefer 2NT (invitational) or 3NT (game) directly

### Opener's Responses

| Response | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| 2D | No 4-card major |
| 2H | 4+ hearts (bids hearts first with 4-4 in majors) |
| 2S | 4+ spades (denies 4 hearts) |

### Responder's Continuations After 2D (No Major)

| Continuation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 2H / 2S | 5-card suit, invitational (pass or raise) |
| 2NT | 8–9 HCP, invitational |
| 3C / 3D | 5+ cards in minor, slam interest |
| 3H / 3S | 5-card suit, game forcing |
| 3NT | 10–15 HCP, to play |

### Responder's Continuations After 2H / 2S (Major Found)

| Continuation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| Raise to 3 | Invitational |
| Raise to 4 | Game; to play |
| 2NT / 3NT | No fit in that major; natural |

### Stayman After Interference

| Situation | Treatment |
|-----------|-----------|
| RHO doubles 1NT | All systems on (Stayman, transfers work normally) |
| RHO bids 2C over 1NT | Double is Stayman; other systems on |
| RHO bids 2D over 1NT | 3D is Stayman; double transfers to hearts |
| RHO bids 2H or higher | Cue bid is Stayman (game forcing); suit bids natural |

### Stayman When Doubled

- Opener makes normal rebid, or passes with 4 clubs, or redoubles with 5 clubs (or 4 good ones)

### Stayman When Overcalled

- Opener bids 4-card major at 2-level, doubles for penalty, or passes
- Responder may double for penalty, pass, or make normal rebid

## Jacoby Transfers

### When They Apply

- Over 1NT opening (15–17)
- Over 2NT opening (20–21)
- Over 2C—2D—2NT (22–24)

### Bids

| Bid | Transfers To |
|-----|-------------|
| 2D | Hearts (opener bids 2H) |
| 2H | Spades (opener bids 2S) |

Over 2NT: 3D transfers to hearts, 3H transfers to spades.

### Requirements

- **5+ cards** in the target major
- **Any strength** — transfers may be used with zero points planning to pass (sign-off in a major)
- Exception: with 5-4 in the majors, may use Stayman instead

### Opener's Acceptance

| Action | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Accept at cheapest level (e.g., 2D→2H) | Normal acceptance; any hand |
| Jump accept (e.g., 2D→3H) | 17 HCP maximum with 4-card support for responder's suit (super-accept) |

### Responder's Continuations After Normal Acceptance

| Continuation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| Pass | Weak hand; sign-off |
| 2NT | 5-card suit, 8–9 HCP; invitational (opener passes, bids 3NT, or bids 3 of major) |
| 3NT | 5-card suit, 10–15 HCP; game values (opener may correct to 4 of major with 3+ support) |
| Raise to 3 of major | 6+ card suit; invitational |
| Raise to 4 of major | 6+ card suit; to play |
| New suit at 3-level | Natural, forcing; shows second suit |

### With 5-5 in the Majors

| Strength | Sequence |
|----------|----------|
| Invitational | Transfer to hearts (2D→2H), then bid 2S |
| Game forcing | Transfer to spades (2H→2S), then bid 3H |

### Transfers After Interference

| Situation | Treatment |
|-----------|-----------|
| Transfer doubled by RHO | Opener may: pass (responder can pass/redouble/bid normally), redouble (strong holding in doubled suit), complete transfer (3+ trumps), or accept at 3-level (4 trumps, maximum) |
| Transfer overcalled by RHO | Opener may: bid 3 of major (good hand), double for penalty, or pass. Responder may re-transfer, bid normally, double for strength, or pass |

## Jacoby 2NT

### When It Applies

In response to a 1H or 1S opening by partner.

### Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Trump support | 4+ cards in opener's major |
| Strength | 13+ dummy points |
| Status | Game forcing |
| By unpassed hand only | A passed hand cannot use Jacoby 2NT |

### Opener's Rebids After Jacoby 2NT

Trump suit is established. Opener describes hand:

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 3-level new suit (e.g., 1H—2NT—3C) | Singleton or void in the bid suit |
| 4-level new suit (e.g., 1H—2NT—4C) | 5+ card side suit (a source of tricks, no shortness) |
| 3 of agreed major (e.g., 1H—2NT—3H) | Maximum (18+), no shortness |
| 3NT | Medium (15–17), no shortness |
| 4 of agreed major (e.g., 1H—2NT—4H) | Minimum (12–14), no shortness |

## Fourth Suit Forcing

### When It Applies

When three suits have been bid naturally and responder bids the **fourth (only remaining unbid) suit** at the 2-level or higher.

### Rules

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Meaning | Forcing for one round; may be artificial |
| Shows | Game-invitational values or better; does not necessarily show length in the fourth suit |
| Purpose | Asks opener to further describe their hand |

### Opener's Responses to Fourth Suit Forcing

| Response | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| Bid responder's first suit with 3 cards | Shows delayed support (jump raise with extra values) |
| Rebid own second suit at cheapest level | Minimum |
| Jump rebid own second suit | Extra values |
| Rebid own first suit at cheapest level | Minimum |
| Jump rebid own first suit | Extra values |
| Bid notrump | Shows stopper in the fourth suit |
| Raise fourth suit with 4 cards | Natural support |

## Jordan 2NT (After Takeout Double)

### When It Applies

When partner opens and RHO makes a takeout double.

### Bid

2NT by responder.

### Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Trump support | 3+ cards in opener's major (or adequate minor support) |
| Strength | Limit raise or better (10+ points) |
| Replaces | The standard limit raise (which is no longer available because a jump raise is preemptive after a double) |

## 2S Puppet to 3C (Over 1NT)

### When It Applies

Over partner's 1NT opening.

### Bid

2S by responder.

### Meaning

Requests opener to bid 3C. Responder then:
- **Passes** with a weak hand and long clubs
- **Bids 3D** with a weak hand and long diamonds

This is the mechanism for signing off in a minor suit over 1NT.

## Interference Over 1NT — Summary

| Opponent's Action | Effect on Conventions |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| RHO doubles | All systems on (Stayman, transfers work normally) |
| RHO bids 2C | Double = Stayman; other systems on |
| RHO bids 2D | 3D = Stayman; Double transfers to hearts |
| RHO bids 2H+ | Cue bid = Stayman (game forcing); suit bids natural |

General principle: **Any bid or double by the opponents cancels a convention intended for non-competitive sequences**, but SAYC specifies the above treatments for interference over 1NT.

## Conventions Not Included in Standard SAYC

The following conventions are **not** part of the official SAYC system (though they are commonly added by partnership agreement):

- Gambling 3NT
- Roman Key Card Blackwood (SAYC uses standard Blackwood)
- New Minor Forcing
- Drury
- Lebensohl
- Inverted minors
- Bergen raises
- Splinter bids
- Texas transfers (sometimes included; not universally part of SAYC)
- Minor suit transfers over 1NT
