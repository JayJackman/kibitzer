# Competitive Bidding

## Overcalls

### Simple Suit Overcall

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| HCP | 8–16 |
| Suit length | 5+ cards (a 1-level major overcall may occasionally be made with an exceptional 4-card suit) |

### Responses to Partner's Overcall

| Response | Requirements |
|----------|-------------|
| Simple raise | 3+ card support, 6–11 points |
| Jump raise | 4+ card support; preemptive (not invitational) |
| Jump to game | 5+ card support; preemptive (10-card fit recommended) |
| 1NT | 9–11 HCP, stopper in opponent's suit, denies fit |
| 2NT (non-jump) | 11–13 HCP, stopper, denies fit |
| 2NT (jump) | 13–15 HCP, stopper |
| 3NT | 15–16 HCP, stopper, to play |
| New suit | Non-forcing; usually denies fit |
| Cue bid of opponent's suit | Limit raise or better in overcaller's suit |

### Overcaller's Rebid After Cue Bid

| Hand | Rebid |
|------|-------|
| Minimum overcall (8–12) | Cheapest rebid (e.g., rebid own suit at cheapest level, bid 2D) |
| Extra strength (13–16) | Jump rebid or higher bid |

## 1NT Overcall

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| HCP | 15–18 (direct position) |
| Shape | Balanced |
| Stopper | Stopper(s) in opponent's suit |
| Systems | Stayman (2C) is on; other conventions may apply by agreement |

### Reopening 1NT (Balancing)

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| HCP | 10–15 (lighter than direct; partner may have been trapped) |
| Shape | Balanced |

## Jump Overcalls

Jump overcalls are **preemptive** in SAYC — same shape and values as an opening bid at that level.

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| (1D)—2S | Weak jump overcall; equivalent to a weak 2S opening |
| (1C)—3H | Equivalent to a preemptive 3H opening |

## Takeout Double

### Requirements

- Used over opening bids through 4D
- Shows support for the unbid suits (especially unbid majors) or a very strong hand
- Typically 13+ dummy points with shortness in opponent's suit

### Responses to Partner's Takeout Double

| Response | Requirements |
|----------|-------------|
| Cheapest bid in a suit | 0–9 points (forced response; does not show strength) |
| 1NT | 6–10 HCP, balanced, stopper in opponent's suit |
| Jump in a suit | 10–12 points; invitational, non-forcing |
| 2NT | 10–12 HCP, balanced, stopper in opponent's suit |
| Cue bid of opponent's suit | Forcing; usually shows opening-strength hand |
| 3NT | 13–16 HCP, stopper; to play |
| Double-jump in a suit | Preemptive |
| Pass (penalty) | Rare; 5+ good cards in opponent's suit; converts takeout to penalty |

### Doubler's Rebid After a Minimum Response

| Rebid | Requirements |
|-------|-------------|
| Pass | 16 or fewer points |
| Raise | 17–18 points, 4+ card support |
| Jump raise | 19–21 points, 4+ card support |
| New suit | 18–20 points, 5+ cards |
| Jump in new suit | 6+ card self-sufficient suit, strong, non-forcing |
| 1NT | 18–20 HCP |
| 2NT (non-jump) | 19–21 HCP |
| 2NT (jump) | 21–22 HCP |
| 3NT | About 9 tricks |
| Cue bid | 21+ points, slam interest |

### Penalty Doubles

- Over opening bids at the 4H level or higher: for penalty (not takeout)
- Penalty double of 1NT: roughly the same shape and strength as the 1NT opener

## Negative Doubles

Negative doubles are used through **2S** (per the SAYC convention card and booklet). Some secondary sources extend this through 3S, but the official SAYC specification is through 2S.

A negative double shows values in the unbid suits, especially unbid majors.

### Specific Negative Double Meanings

| Auction | Negative Double Shows |
|---------|----------------------|
| 1C—(1D)—Dbl | 4-4 or better in both majors |
| 1D—(1H)—Dbl | Exactly 4 spades (5+ spades would bid 1S directly) |
| 1D—(1S)—Dbl | 4+ hearts |
| 1C—(1H)—Dbl | 4+ spades |
| 1C—(1S)—Dbl | 4+ hearts |
| 1H—(1S)—Dbl | Typically 4+ cards in minors, some values |
| Higher-level | 4+ cards in unbid major(s) |

### Minimum HCP for Negative Doubles

- At the 1-level: approximately 6+ HCP
- At the 2-level: approximately 8+ HCP
- At the 3-level: approximately 10+ HCP

### Opener's Rebid After a Negative Double

| Rebid | Requirements |
|-------|-------------|
| Cheapest level in unbid suit | Minimum (12–15), non-forcing |
| Jump in unbid suit | 16–18 points; non-forcing, invitational |
| Cue bid | 19+ points; game forcing |
| Pass | Rare; for penalty; good holding in opponent's suit |

## Michaels Cue Bid

A direct cue bid of the opponent's opening suit shows a two-suited hand (5-5 or more distributional).

### Over Minor Suit Openings

| Auction | Shows |
|---------|-------|
| (1C)—2C | 5-5 or better in **both majors** |
| (1D)—2D | 5-5 or better in **both majors** |

### Over Major Suit Openings

| Auction | Shows |
|---------|-------|
| (1H)—2H | 5+ spades and 5+ in an **unspecified minor** |
| (1S)—2S | 5+ hearts and 5+ in an **unspecified minor** |

### Strength

Michaels shows either:
- A **weak** hand (less than opening values), or
- A **strong** hand (17+ points, planning to bid again)

Intermediate hands (opening values without extras) should overcall rather than use Michaels.

### Responses to Michaels

| Response | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| Bid a known major | To play, choosing the major |
| 2NT (over major Michaels) | Asks partner to bid their minor |
| 3C / 3D (over major Michaels) | Preference if minor is known |
| Raise to game | Preemptive or to play |

## Unusual 2NT

A **jump** to 2NT over an opponent's opening bid shows a two-suited hand in the two **lowest-ranking unbid suits** (5-5 or better).

| Auction | Shows |
|---------|-------|
| (1H)—2NT | 5-5+ in clubs and diamonds |
| (1S)—2NT | 5-5+ in clubs and diamonds |
| (1C)—2NT | 5-5+ in diamonds and hearts |
| (1D)—2NT | 5-5+ in clubs and hearts |

### Strength

Same as Michaels: weak (less than opening values) or strong (17+ for further action). Intermediate hands should overcall.

### Over Weak Twos

A 2NT overcall of a weak two shows 16–19 HCP, balanced, with stopper in opponent's suit (this is **natural**, not Unusual).

## Balancing (4th Seat / Reopening)

When the auction is about to die (e.g., 1H—Pass—Pass), the balancing seat may act with lighter values since partner may have been trapped with a good hand.

| Action | Requirements |
|--------|-------------|
| 1NT | 10–15 HCP, balanced |
| Simple suit bid | 8–14 points, 5+ cards |
| Jump in suit | Opening-strength hand |
| Double | Takeout; may be lighter than direct |
| Double then 2NT | 16–17 HCP |
| Jump to 2NT | 18–20 HCP |
| Cue bid | Takeout; shows void in opponent's suit |

---

## After Opponent's Takeout Double

When partner opens and RHO doubles:

| Response | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| 1-level suit bid | Forcing for one round (unlimited points) |
| 2-level new suit (below game) | Non-forcing; usually 6 cards, 6–10 points |
| 2NT — Jordan | Limit raise or better in opener's suit (replaces the standard limit raise) |
| Jump raise (e.g., 1H—Dbl—3H) | Preemptive; good support, fewer than 10 HCP |
| Redouble | 10+ HCP; tends to deny fit; interest in penalizing opponents |
| Jump shift | Preemptive (weak jump shift in competition) |

## General Competitive Principles

- **Bids mean the same thing they meant without the intervening bid** (the general SAYC principle for competition)
- **Cue bid of RHO's overcall** by responder: game forcing; asks for further clarification; typically a game-forcing raise
- **Any bid or double by the opponents cancels a convention intended for non-competitive sequences** (e.g., if opponents bid over 1NT, some conventions may no longer apply — specifics in [05-conventions.md](05-conventions.md))
- **Redouble meanings vary by context**:
  - At 4-level or higher: for penalty (to play)
  - After opponent doubles an artificial bid: to play
  - After opponent's takeout double: shows 10+ HCP
  - At 3-level or lower when doubled in a trump suit: SOS (requesting a different suit)
