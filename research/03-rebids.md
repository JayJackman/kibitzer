# Rebids

## Opener's Rebids

### Strength Categories

| Category | Points | Actions |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Minimum | 12–16 | Non-jump rebids; cheapest level |
| Medium | 17–18 | Jump rebids; reverses |
| Maximum | 19–21 | Double-jump rebids; jump shifts |

---

### After a 1-Level Response in a New Suit

#### Minimum (12–16 points)

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Raise responder's suit at cheapest level | 4-card support, minimum |
| Rebid own suit at cheapest level | 6+ cards, minimum |
| Bid new suit (non-reverse) at cheapest level | 4+ cards, new suit ranks lower than first suit |
| 1NT | 12–14 HCP, balanced, no other descriptive bid |

#### Medium (17–18 points)

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Jump raise responder's suit | 4-card support, 17–18 points |
| Jump rebid own suit | 6+ cards, 17–18 points |
| Reverse (new suit ranking higher than first, at 2-level) | First suit longer than second; 17+ points; forcing one round |

**Reverse**: A bid in a new suit that ranks higher than the opening suit at the 2-level (e.g., 1D—1S—2H). Requires extra strength (17+ points) because it forces responder to the 3-level to return to opener's first suit. First suit must be longer than the second.

#### Maximum (19–21 points)

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Jump in notrump (e.g., 1H—1S—2NT) | 18–19 HCP, balanced |
| Double jump raise responder's suit | 4-card support, 19–21 points |
| Double jump rebid own suit | Self-supporting 6+ card suit, 19–21 points |
| Jump shift into second suit | 4+ cards in new suit, 19–21 points; forcing |

---

### After a 2-Over-1 Response (e.g., 1H—2C)

The 2-over-1 response promises 10+ points and a rebid (unless opener jumps to game). Opener rebids naturally:

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Cheapest NT | Balanced minimum (12–14) |
| Raise responder's suit | 4-card support |
| Rebid own suit | 6+ cards |
| New suit | 4+ cards, natural |
| Jump rebid | Extra values |

---

### After a Single Raise (e.g., 1H—2H)

Responder has shown 6–10 dummy points with 3+ card support.

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Pass | Minimum (12–15), content with partscore |
| 3H/3S (raise to 3) | Invitational, ~16–18 points; asks responder to bid game with a maximum |
| 4H/4S (raise to game) | 19+ points; enough for game opposite a minimum raise |
| New suit | Natural, game try; asks responder about help in that suit |

---

### After a 1NT Response

Responder has shown 6–10 HCP, no fit, no biddable suit at the 1-level.

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Pass | Balanced minimum |
| 2 of a lower new suit | 4+ cards; non-forcing |
| 2 of original suit | 6+ cards; non-forcing |
| 2NT | 18–19 HCP, balanced; invitational |
| 3 of original suit | 6+ cards, 17–18 points; invitational |
| Jump in new suit | 19+ points, 4+ cards; forcing |
| 3NT | 19–21 HCP, balanced |

---

### After a Limit Raise (e.g., 1H—3H)

Responder has shown 10–12 dummy points with 3+ card support. Invitational.

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Pass | Minimum (12–14), decline the invitation |
| 4H/4S | Accept the invitation; bid game |

---

### After Jacoby 2NT (e.g., 1H—2NT)

Game-forcing raise with 4+ trumps and 13+ dummy points. Trump suit is established.

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 3-level new suit (3C/3D/3S) | Singleton or void in the bid suit |
| 4-level new suit (4C/4D/4S) | 5+ card side suit (a source of tricks) |
| 3 of agreed major (e.g., 3H) | Maximum (18+), no shortness |
| 3NT | Medium (15–17), no shortness |
| 4 of agreed major (e.g., 4H) | Minimum (12–14), no shortness |

---

### After a Jump Shift (e.g., 1H—2S or 1H—3C)

Responder shows 19+ points and slam interest. Game forcing.

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Raise responder's suit | 4-card support |
| Rebid own suit | 6+ cards |
| New suit | Natural |
| Notrump | Balanced, stoppers |

Opener should describe their hand; slam investigation continues.

## Responder's Rebids

### Sign-off (Partscore) Actions

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Pass | Content with the current contract |
| 1NT | Minimum response, no fit |
| 2-level in previously mentioned suit | Preference; non-forcing |

### Invitational Actions

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 2NT | ~11–12 HCP; invitational to 3NT |
| 3-level in previously mentioned suit | Invitational (e.g., 1H—1S—2C—3C) |

### Forcing Actions

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| New suit (at 2-level or higher) | Forcing one round |
| Jump raise of opener's major | Game forcing |
| Fourth suit bid | Forcing one round, may be artificial (see [05-conventions.md](05-conventions.md)) |
| Reverse or jump shift after 1NT rebid | Forcing to game |

### Key Principles

- A 2-over-1 response promises a rebid unless opener jumps to game
- Responder's jump raises of a major are game forcing
- Responder's preference bids (returning to opener's first suit at the cheapest level) are non-forcing
- New suits by responder on the second round are forcing for one round
- Fourth suit forcing: a bid of the only unbid suit at the 2-level or higher is forcing for one round and may be artificial (see [05-conventions.md](05-conventions.md))
