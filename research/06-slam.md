# Slam Bidding

## Blackwood (4NT)

### When It Applies

4NT is Blackwood (ace-asking) in **suit auctions** — when a trump suit has been agreed or strongly implied.

**Important**: SAYC uses **standard Blackwood**, not Roman Key Card Blackwood.

### Responses to 4NT

| Response | Aces Held |
|----------|-----------|
| 5C | 0 or 4 aces |
| 5D | 1 ace |
| 5H | 2 aces |
| 5S | 3 aces |

### 5NT King Ask (Follow-up)

After receiving the ace response, the Blackwood bidder may bid 5NT to ask for kings. Bidding 5NT **guarantees the partnership holds all four aces**.

| Response | Kings Held |
|----------|------------|
| 6C | 0 or 4 kings |
| 6D | 1 king |
| 6H | 2 kings |
| 6S | 3 kings |

### When NOT to Use Blackwood

- With a void (aces cannot be accurately counted; prefer cue bidding)
- When the response may carry the auction too high (e.g., if the 5-level response bypasses the trump suit)
- When the combined values are unclear — Blackwood checks for aces, not overall strength

## Gerber (4C)

### When It Applies

4C is Gerber (ace-asking) over **notrump bids**. Specifically:

- Over a **1NT opening**
- Over a **2NT opening**
- Over any **rebid of 1NT or 2NT** by opener
- Over **2C—2D—2NT** (22–24)
- Over **2C—2D—3NT** (25–27)

### Responses to 4C (Gerber)

| Response | Aces Held |
|----------|-----------|
| 4D | 0 or 4 aces |
| 4H | 1 ace |
| 4S | 2 aces |
| 4NT | 3 aces |

### 5C King Ask (Follow-up)

After the ace response, 5C asks for kings:

| Response | Kings Held |
|----------|------------|
| 5D | 0 or 4 kings |
| 5H | 1 king |
| 5S | 2 kings |
| 5NT | 3 kings |

### Distinguishing Gerber from a Natural 4C

4C is Gerber only when bid **directly over a notrump bid or rebid**. In other contexts (e.g., 1H—2C—3C—4C), 4C is a natural raise.

## Quantitative 4NT

### When It Applies

4NT is **quantitative** (not Blackwood) when bid **directly over a notrump bid** and no trump suit has been agreed.

| Example Auctions | Meaning |
|-----------------|---------|
| 1NT—4NT | Quantitative; invites 6NT |
| 2NT—4NT | Quantitative; invites 6NT |
| 1NT—2C—2D—4NT | Quantitative (Stayman found no fit) |

### Opener's Response

| Action | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Pass | Minimum; decline slam invitation |
| 6NT | Maximum; accept slam invitation |

## Grand Slam Force (5NT)

### When It Applies

A **jump to 5NT** in a suit auction (not after Blackwood 4NT) asks partner about top trump honors.

### Requirements

The 5NT bidder is asking: "Do you hold two of the three top trump honors (Ace, King, Queen)?"

### Partner's Response

| Response | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| 6 of trump suit | Fewer than two of the top three honors |
| 7 of trump suit | Two of the three top honors (A, K, Q) |

## Cue Bidding (Control-Showing Bids)

### When They Apply

Once a trump suit has been agreed, bids in new suits at the 4-level (or sometimes 3-level) may be **cue bids** showing controls (aces, kings, voids, or singletons) in that suit.

### Rules

- Cue bids are used to investigate slam when Blackwood is not appropriate (e.g., when holding a void)
- Cue bids are typically made **up the line** — bid the cheapest control first
- Bypassing a suit implies no control in that suit
- Cue bids are forcing — the partnership continues until a slam is bid or the agreed trump suit is bid at the minimum level

### Example

```
1H — 2NT (Jacoby 2NT, hearts agreed)
3C (singleton club) — 3D (cue bid, diamond control)
3S (cue bid, spade control) — 4NT (Blackwood, having shown controls)
```

## Interference Over Blackwood / Gerber

### DOPI (Double = 0, Pass = 1)

When an opponent bids over Blackwood (4NT) or Gerber (4C):

| Action | Aces Held |
|--------|-----------|
| Double | 0 aces |
| Pass | 1 ace |
| Next step bid | 2 aces |
| Next + 1 bid | 3 aces |

### ROPI (Redouble = 0, Pass = 1)

When an opponent **doubles** Blackwood or Gerber:

| Action | Aces Held |
|--------|-----------|
| Redouble | 0 aces |
| Pass | 1 ace |
| Next step bid | 2 aces |
| Next + 1 bid | 3 aces |

## Slam Bidding Guidelines

### When to Consider Slam

| Points (Combined) | Target |
|-------------------|--------|
| 33+ HCP | 6NT (small slam in notrump) |
| 37+ HCP | 7NT (grand slam in notrump) |
| 33+ total points (suit) | Small slam in a suit |

### Slam Investigation Tools — Summary

| Tool | When to Use |
|------|-------------|
| Blackwood (4NT) | Suit auction; need to check for missing aces |
| Gerber (4C) | Notrump auction; need to check for missing aces |
| Quantitative 4NT | Notrump auction; invite slam based on point count |
| Grand Slam Force (5NT) | Need to check for top trump honors specifically |
| Cue bidding | Trump agreed; need to show specific controls; void in hand makes Blackwood unreliable |
